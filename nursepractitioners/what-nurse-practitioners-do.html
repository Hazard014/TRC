Advanced Practice Nurses with Comprehensive Healthcare Authority

Nurse practitioners are advanced practice nurses (APNs) with Master's-level education who provide comprehensive healthcare services. They hold independent prescribing authority and can diagnose illnesses, order diagnostic tests, and manage complete treatment plans across all provinces and territories.

Diagnose Illnesses

Comprehensive health assessment, differential diagnosis, and clinical decision-making for acute and chronic conditions

Order & Interpret Diagnostic Tests

Laboratory work, imaging studies, diagnostic procedures, and result interpretation for patient care

Prescribe Medications

Independent prescriptive authority for medications, treatments, and therapeutic interventions

Manage Treatment Plans

Complete patient care management including treatment planning, follow-up care, and outcome monitoring

Preventive Care Services

Health promotion, disease prevention, patient education, and wellness counseling

Practice Settings

  • Primary Care: Family practice, community health centers, urgent care, walk-in clinics
  • Specialty Areas: Oncology, cardiology, women's health, pediatrics, geriatrics, mental health
  • Independent Practice: Private practice clinics, collaborative care models, telehealth services
  • Acute Care: Hospital-based NP roles, emergency departments, specialized units

NP Autonomy: Nurse practitioners work independently in all provinces and territories with varying levels of practice autonomy and billing privileges depending on regional regulations.
