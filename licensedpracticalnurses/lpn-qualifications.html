All Our LPNs Meet Provincial Standards

Every licensed practical nurse in our network holds proper education, licensing, and clinical competencies

Diploma in Practical Nursing

2-year diploma program from accredited college providing focused nursing education and extensive clinical practice

Key Components: Anatomy & physiology • Pharmacology • Nursing procedures • Clinical placements • Patient care skills • Healthcare ethics

Clinical Training

Extensive hands-on clinical experience in various healthcare settings including long-term care, acute care, and community health

Competencies: Patient assessment • Medication administration • Wound care • Infection control • Documentation

REx-PN Examination (BC & Ontario)

Practical nurse registration examination implemented in BC and Ontario since 2022, replacing CPNRE

Validation: Computer-based test ensuring competency in safe, effective practical nursing practice

CPNRE Examination (Other Provinces)

Canadian Practical Nurse Registration Examination for provinces that haven't transitioned to REx-PN

Recognition: National standard ensuring consistent competency across Canada

Provincial Registration

Active registration with relevant provincial regulatory body (CLPNA in Alberta, CNO in Ontario, BCCNM in BC)

Verification: Current registration status verified before each placement

Regional Variations Covered

  • Ontario: Called Registered Practical Nurses (RPNs) — same scope and competencies
  • Quebec: Uses different licensing exam and education model (OIIQ system)
  • Western Provinces: Expanding scope of practice with more independent decision-making
