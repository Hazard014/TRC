Focused Care for Stable and Predictable Patients

Our licensed practical nurses hold nursing diplomas and provide essential nursing care for patients with stable conditions. They work under supervision or in consultation with RNs and NPs, delivering skilled care with significant autonomy in Alberta and BC.

Stable Patient Care

Comprehensive care for patients with predictable conditions including post-surgical, chronic disease, and rehabilitation patients.

Wound Care Management

Advanced wound assessment, dressing changes, wound care protocols, and healing progress monitoring.

Medication Administration

Safe medication administration, dosage calculations, patient education, and medication compliance monitoring.

Chronic Disease Management

Diabetes care, hypertension monitoring, COPD management, and other chronic condition support.

Collaboration with Healthcare Teams

Work under supervision or consultation with RNs and NPs, coordinate with interdisciplinary teams.

Regional Practice Variations

  • BC & Alberta: Significant autonomy with expanded scope including independent decision-making in some acute care settings.
  • Ontario: Called Registered Practical Nurses (RPNs) with similar scope of practice.
  • All Provinces: Task-based focus with increasing responsibilities and scope expansion.

Work Environments

Our LPNs work in long-term care facilities, chronic care units, rehabilitation centers, community health, home care, and increasingly in acute care settings.
