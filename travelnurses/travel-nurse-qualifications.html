Provincial Registration Requirements for Travel Nurses

Understanding the licensing requirements for temporary nursing assignments across provinces

Licensing Requirements

  • Home Province Licensure: Must hold active license in the province or territory where they intend to work, even temporarily
  • Process: Cannot practice without proper provincial registration regardless of assignment duration
  • Verification: We verify active registration status for all travel nursing candidates

Cross-Provincial Work Challenges

  • No National License: Canada does not have a national nursing license system (unlike the U.S. compact model)
  • Provincial Applications: Travel nurses must apply for licensure in each province/territory where they accept assignments
  • Example: An RN licensed in Ontario must apply for temporary or full registration in BC to work as a travel nurse

BC-Specific Requirements

  • BCCNM Registration: All nurses working in BC must register with BC College of Nurses and Midwives
  • Temporary Registration: Available for short-term assignments while maintaining home province license
  • Expedited Process: Streamlined application for qualified nurses from other provinces

Our Registration Support

  • Application Assistance: Guidance through provincial registration processes
  • Documentation Support: Help with required paperwork and credential transfers
  • Timeline Management: Coordination of registration timing with assignment start dates
  • Compliance Verification: Ensuring all regulatory requirements are met before placement
