Short-Term Healthcare Assignments for Temporary Staffing Needs

Travel nurses are licensed nurses (RNs, LPNs, or NPs) who take short-term assignments—typically 8–26 weeks—in locations experiencing workforce shortages. They provide essential flexibility for healthcare facilities with temporary staffing needs.

Temporary Assignments

Contract periods ranging from 8–26 weeks for specific staffing needs

Emergency Coverage

Rapid deployment for unexpected staffing shortages or crisis situations

Rural Placements

Coverage for remote communities and underserved healthcare regions

Seasonal Support

Additional staffing for high-demand periods or special projects

Specialized Projects

Coverage during facility expansions, renovations, or new service launches

Important Clarification

Travel nursing is not a separate licensure class—all travel nurses must hold active licensure (RN, LPN, NP) with Canadian nursing regulatory bodies and meet provincial registration requirements.

Travel Nursing Applications

  • Hospital Coverage: Emergency departments, ICU, medical-surgical units during staff shortages
  • Long-Term Care: Temporary coverage for extended care facilities and rehabilitation centers
  • Community Health: Rural health stations and northern Indigenous health programs
  • Project-Based Work: New facility openings, special programs, and seasonal demand increases
