Specialized Mental Health, Addiction, and Developmental Health Experts

Psychiatric nurses (RPNs) are specialized mental health professionals distinct from general RPNs in Ontario. They focus exclusively on mental health, addictions, and developmental health with specialized education and training in psychiatric care.

Important Distinction: Psychiatric RPNs are completely different from Registered Practical Nurses (RPNs) in Ontario who are equivalent to LPNs elsewhere. Psychiatric nurses are a specialized mental health designation available only in western provinces.

Mental Health Assessment

Comprehensive psychiatric assessments, mental status exams, risk evaluations, and treatment planning.

Crisis Intervention

De-escalation, stabilization, psychiatric emergencies, and safety management protocols.

Addiction Treatment

Assessment, withdrawal support, addiction counseling, and recovery programming.

Psychotropic Medication Management

Administration, side effect monitoring, and medication education for patients.

Therapeutic Interventions

Supportive therapy, behavioral interventions, therapeutic communication, and milieu therapy.

Specialized Practice Areas

  • Psychiatric Hospitals: Inpatient units, acute care, long-term psychiatric facilities
  • Addiction Treatment Centers: Detox, rehab, outpatient programs
  • Forensic Settings: Correctional facilities, court-ordered assessments
  • Community Mental Health: Crisis teams, outreach, community clinics
  • Developmental Health: Dual diagnosis support and behavioral management
